01

June 19 · Foundation

Who We Are —
Stories Behind the Screens

Belonging · Identity · Background

Core value thread: Virtue First · Service

To open (5 min)

Start here — everyone answers

"Take 30 seconds to think of one word that describes how you showed up to work this week. Not good or bad, just honest."

Go around once quickly — just the word, no explanation needed. This gets every voice in the room early and sets a tone of honesty over performance. There are no wrong answers.

Discussion questions (30–40 min)

You won't need all five — pick what resonates with your group. Going deep on two or three is better than skimming all of them.

Q1 · Warmup

What's one thing about where you grew up that still shows up in how you work?

Virtue First

Q2 · Career path

What's the most unexpected job or role you held before this one — and what did it teach you?

Q3 · Values

Think of someone who shaped how you work or show up for others. What's one thing you absorbed from them — maybe without even realizing it?

Service

Q4 · Pride

What's something you're genuinely proud of that has nothing to do with your job title?

Q5 · Honest ground

What's something that keeps you going on the hard days — at work or outside of it? It doesn't have to be inspirational, just true.

Virtue First

To close (5–10 min)

Closing round

Each person shares one thing they learned about someone else in the group today that surprised them. Be specific — not "I learned a lot" but "I didn't know that Maya had worked in physical therapy before tech."

✦ Invitation

Share your personal mission statement

At Players Health, we each carry a personal mission — the reason we show up. If you feel comfortable, share yours with the group. If you haven't written one yet, try finishing this sentence out loud: "I am at my best when I'm…"

If your group surfaces something worth sharing more widely — a connection, an idea, a moment — bring it to the team in Slack.

💬 Share back with HR

Did a question spark a great idea or conversation worth keeping? Drop it in #summer-cohorts on Slack — the People team reads every post and uses your input to shape future cohorts.

How These Sessions Work

This guide is for everyone in the cohort — not just whoever is running the conversation. The more everyone understands the structure, the better the discussion tends to go.

Opening ritual

0:05 – 0:40

Close + mission

Ground rules — share these at the start of each session

🔒 What's shared stays here

These conversations are confidential within the group. What someone shares here isn't for the broader org — unless they choose to share it themselves.

🎙 One voice at a time

Let people finish. Listen to understand, not to respond. You don't need to fix or solve what someone shares.

✋ Pass is always okay

No one has to answer any question. If something doesn't feel right to share, a simple "I'll pass on this one" is completely respected.

⏱ Depth over coverage

You don't need to use every question. A rich conversation on two questions is worth more than surface-level answers on all five.

Tips for whoever is leading the session

Go first when things are quiet. Vulnerability is contagious. If you share something real, others will follow.
Name what you're noticing. "I'm hearing a common theme here…" helps people feel seen and moves the conversation forward.
If one voice is dominating, gently redirect: "I'd love to hear from someone we haven't heard from yet."
Follow the energy, not the script. If a question goes somewhere unexpected and real, stay there. The guide is a map, not a mandate.
Hold space for where people actually are. Some folks are having a hard season. Honor that. The goal is honest connection, not performed positivity.
After Session 4, consider sending a short personal note to each person in your cohort — one specific thing you noticed or appreciated about them across the summer.
